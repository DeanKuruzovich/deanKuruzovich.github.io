Robotic Arms

Throughout robotics club, I have been interested in robotic arms, which is why I began building one. My end goal has been to create a cheap, 3D-printable robotic arm using inexpensive motors rather than stepper motors or servos, which are typically used but are often significantly more expensive and have worse power ratings. This approach also allows me to design a custom control system.

V1

This was my first design of a robotic arm. It was experimental and had many flaws, but it allowed me to explore ideas like a custom 3D-printed planetary gearbox and mounting electronics on the arm to reduce wiring and avoid a large external driver board. The arm was driven by a Raspberry Pi 3 with the intention of using the camera module and AprilTags for control rather than external encoders.

This is the gripper I designed. It is not powerful enough to do much as it uses a small 9-gram servo.

Before the second iteration, I built a mini arm using potentiometers to measure joint positions on a roughly 10 cm tall 3D-printed arm. The potentiometers are read by an Arduino Uno which can communicate with a larger arm driver for calibration potential training of future autonomous control systems.
